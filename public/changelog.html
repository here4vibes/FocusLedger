July 2026
18 Jul
Weightless mode — one conversation, surfaces summoned
A new conversation-first way to use FocusLedger, living at /weightless alongside the classic app. Buddy greets you with just what's actually on your radar — not the whole pile — and everything else (your tasks, your money, what you're stuck on) gets summoned into the chat only when it's relevant, then gets out of the way. Talking to Buddy writes straight to your ledger: mention you finished something and it's marked done; mention a purchase and it's logged — each shown as a small receipt so you can see it land. This maps to cognitive load from the science page — a single low-friction surface means less working memory spent navigating and more left for actually doing.

13 Jul
Password reset & account emails fixed
Password-reset, account-deletion, welcome, and Pro-welcome emails had silently stopped sending — the templates behind them had gone missing, so those flows errored out before an email ever left. All are restored with a cleaner, on-brand design, and a new automated guard now catches this whole class of "imported-but-undefined" bug before it can ship again.

13 Jul
Frosted-glass bottom navigation
The floating bottom nav pill now uses a translucent frosted-glass effect — content softly blurs behind it as you scroll, the same modern treatment you'll recognize from Instagram and Reddit. A solid fallback keeps the labels crisp on the rare browser without blur support.
Design
13 Jul
Today — your whole day on one timeline
A new Today view (menu → Today) lays your tasks, calendar events, and focus blocks on a single vertical timeline with a live "now" line — answering the two questions ADHD brains ask all day: what am I supposed to be doing right now, and how much day is left? Task blocks are sized by your track record, not your optimism: if tasks usually take you 1.6× your estimate, the blocks render 1.6× bigger, with a chip explaining why. This maps to time blindness research from the science page — externalizing time visually is the single most effective prosthetic for it.
New
12 Jul
Security page — exactly how your money data is protected
A new /security page states our practices specifically, not vaguely: bank credentials never touch our servers (Plaid handles login, read-only access), Plaid tokens encrypted with AES-256-GCM at rest, payments through Stripe's hosted checkout so card numbers never reach us, PBKDF2-SHA512 password hashing, single-use expiring reset links, self-service account deletion, and analytics that re-scramble daily so nobody — including us — can track you across days.
New
12 Jul
Bug reports get an instant, human receipt
Submit a bug report or contact message and you'll get an acknowledgement within seconds — not a ticket number, a note from the founder: your report was read, bug fixes jump the queue, and you'll hear back when it's resolved. During beta, most reported bugs are fixed within a day or two.

09 Jul
Streak-freeze — one missed day no longer resets your streak
Routine streaks now carry a "freeze" that automatically forgives a single missed day, so one slip doesn't wipe out weeks of progress. When a freeze saves your streak, Buddy tells you plainly — "Missed a day, I saved your streak. Still going" — no guilt, no reset to zero. Freezes replenish every 7 days in a row. This maps to the shame-cycle research on the science page — for ADHD brains it's the all-or-nothing collapse after one miss, not the miss itself, that ends a habit.

A plan-the-day prompt only helps if it lands when you can actually act on it. This maps to circadian and energy-management research on the science page — timing a cue to your natural activation window beats fighting it.

Consistency between what you did and what the app remembers is the foundation of trust in a money habit.

June 2026
16 Jun
Habit stacking — anchor tasks to routines + Buddy detects co-occurring task pairs
Tasks can now declare "do this after [Routine]" — open any task in the expanded detail view and pick a routine from the "Do after" dropdown. When you mark that routine complete, FocusLedger sends a push notification reminding you to do the anchored task next. Separately, the weekly pattern detection job now scans your task history for sequential pairs: if you complete Task B within 60 minutes of Task A on 3 or more distinct days, Buddy surfaces a "Habit stack detected 🔗" card with a one-tap "Stack them" to accept it. This maps to implementation intentions from the science page — stimulus-response links that route around the executive function gap and make follow-through automatic.
New
16 Jun
Habit stacking — "when/then" triggers + Morning Essentials routine
After your morning plan is accepted, Buddy now asks you to link each task to something you already do — "After I make my coffee, I'll do [Task]." These implementation intentions are stored against your plan so you can build on them over time. New users also get a pre-built Morning Essentials routine (water, medication, brush teeth, wash face, get dressed, eat something) waiting in their account on day one. Existing users without a morning routine see a one-tap card in Buddy to add it. This maps to implementation intentions from the science page — when-then planning (Gollwitzer) doubles task completion rates by creating automatic stimulus-response links that bypass the executive function gap.
New
16 Jun
Security — Plaid webhook signature verification
Bank transaction webhooks from Plaid are now cryptographically verified before processing. Each webhook carries an ES256-signed JWT; the app validates the signature against Plaid's public key, checks the body hash, and rejects anything older than 5 minutes. Previously any POST to the webhook URL could trigger a bank sync — now only genuine Plaid events are processed.

Focus Mode — Pomodoro ring timer + Google Calendar sync on day view

Added Pomodoro mode (🍅 toggle): 25-minute work blocks auto-advance to a 5-minute break then back to work, tracking how many cycles you've completed. The Calendar page now shows a "Connect Google Calendar" bar — authorize once and your calendar events appear as blue blocks on the timeline so you can schedule around real commitments without switching apps.

Money page — logging now confirms reliably, expenses errors are no longer silent
Logging a spend now confirms with a "✓ Logged!" the moment it saves, even if the dashboard refresh afterward hiccups — previously a refresh glitch could make a successfully-saved expense look like it failed. The expense list also now shows the real reason when it can't load (instead of a generic "Could not load expenses"), and the Refresh button on the bank card works even when an account id is missing. This reduces the "did that actually save?" uncertainty that makes money tracking feel untrustworthy — consistency and immediate feedback are what keep an ADHD-friendly habit loop intact.

09 Jun
Money dashboard unlock — race condition fix + DB-backed unlock state
Fixed a race condition where the "This Week" (day 3) and "Top Categories" (day 7) dashboard cards would never unlock because the dashboard init loaded them in parallel before the first-transaction date was seeded. Unlock state now fetches your actual first expense date directly from the database (instead of a localStorage estimate), so the progress bars are accurate across devices and after cache clears. Evening Swipe unlock also now cross-references triaged expense history from the DB rather than localStorage alone.
