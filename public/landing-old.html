The system

Three steps to owning your day

Not a to-do app. A command center for how your brain actually works.

1

Morning launch — 2 minutes

Pick your mood. Write your 3 priorities. Launch. That 30-second decision at 7am is what separates a controlled morning from a reactive one.

2

Track through the day — 30 seconds

Add spending as it happens. Add tasks when they hit you. Add journal notes at night. No friction. No review sessions. No weekly planning ceremonies.

3

Weekly reset — Sunday, 15 minutes

See what you spent. See what got done. See where you went off-plan. Align it with what actually matters to you. Start next week intentional.

Values first

Your money and time go where your values are

Most budgeting fails because it starts with numbers. FocusLedger starts with what matters to you.

🎯

Values alignment check-in

Every journal entry asks: did today reflect what you care about?

You're not just tracking spending — you're tracking whether your spending reflects your priorities. That $200 on takeout wasn't bad. But if your value is "cook more at home" — that's data. That's a conversation waiting to happen.

Example

You value "Quality time with family." Your spending this month: $340 on restaurants, $0 on a family board game night. FocusLedger doesn't judge. It just asks: is this working?
